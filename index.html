Gender Studies & Intersectionality: Understanding Gender and Class Dynamics with Policies (GSDP 2024)

We are delighted to announce the upcoming conference aiming at the facets of gender dynamics, specifically focusing on women's experiences, challenges and triumphs in the 21st Century Anthropocene. Through insightful discussions, the conference aims to shed light on women's social, cultural and economic empowerment, highlighting the entrepreneurial spirit, resilience and achievements of women in various spheres. With its rich cultural heritage and historical significance, Bihar provides a unique backdrop for exploring gender issues. The state has witnessed remarkable strides in the recent years, with initiatives such as entrepreneurship development programs for women to create economic independence and empowerment.
However, challenges persist, including the need to promote Gender Equality and challenge Binary Paradigms that restrict women's freedom and agency. Gender Report Card 2019 highlights the position of women and girls in the state today and suggests areas that require further efforts for development. Moreover, there is a cavity of genuine knowledge about the Non-binaries, Transgenders and the different gender identities that exist within the social matrices. This conference provides a platform for scholars, activists, policymakers and practitioners to come together and engage in meaningful dialogue on gender issues across the globe, through a series of paper presentations, interactive sessions and insightful discussions. The conference looks forward to exploring innovative approaches to gender equality and women empowerment. Literature has been a powerful tool for challenging societal norms and a medium through which society explores complex themes and ideas, often reflecting and challenging prevailing values. Gender Studies cover a wide spectrum of literary expressions and have remained integral elements of World Literature, from the classical to the modern age. From providing pure delight, entertainment and social criticism to drawing attention to the fundamental incongruities and iniquities of life, writers have deployed their faculties with great effect. It is extraordinary to witness how this genre has seamlessly been incorporated as elements of narrative, characterisation, amplification and subversion, within the accepted conventions of various literary genres. It promises to be an intellectually invigorating and transformative event by deconstructing and reconstructing gender identities by traversing through an intersectional lens, to see how factors like race, class and disability create a complex web of challenges also for LGBTQ+ employees. A transgender woman of colour may face not only discrimination based on her gender identity but also racism and economic marginalization that compound those struggles. Examining these intersections allows us to develop inclusive policies and practices. The academic engagement in this horizon reflects the importance of disseminating targeted education and skill-building programs that cater to the needs of all genders, with a focus on business management, financial literacy and leadership skills.

Speakers

speaker Details

Prof. Maria-Ana Tupan, Romanian professor, literary critic and translator. Member of the Writers' Union of Romania and several international scientific societies. Tupan taught at the University of Bucharest and was a Fulbright visiting professor at Pennsylvania State University in 1994-1995. Later, she completed her habilitation thesis and began supervising PhD students at Alba Iulia University. Member of the Writers' Union of Romania since 1996, Tupan is also part of the German Association for Research in the Fantastic and The Charles Brockden Brown Society.

speaker Details

Dr. Milton Biswas: An academic, educationist, researcher and Professor at Jagannath University, Dhaka, Bangladesh. Also a Bengali writer, columnist, literary critic and media personality. Had been Lecturer, Assistant Professor and Associate Professor at, the Department of Bangla, Chittagong University, Chittagong, Bangladesh, from 2000 to 2013. Currently the Chairman, Bangla Department, Jagannath University, Dhaka, Bangladesh.

speaker Details

Dr. Mehak Burza is one of the Board of Directors and the Head of Global Holocaust and Religious Studies as well as the program director of Holocaust Studies at The Global Center for Religious Research (Denver, United States). As an Assistant Professor of English, she has taught in various colleges of the University of Delhi. She was a selected scholar-in-residence for the ISGAP antisemitism workshop held at the University of Oxford.

speaker Details

Dr. Ranjan Kumar Ghosh is a faculty member and chairperson at the Centre for Management in Agriculture, Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad. His research revolves around institutional economics, sustainable agri-food systems and natural resources policy. He is a regular consultant and advisor to several national and international agencies such as the Sustainable Development Solutions Network (UN-SDSN), The Nature Conservancy (TNS), the Food Systems Economics Commission, WHO, the Environmental Defence Fund, the European Union, CGIAR Global, Ministry of Agriculture, FSSAI and Government of Gujarat. He also sits on the board of a large public sector enterprise, GNFC and Wheels Global Foundation (WGF) Water Council.

speaker Details

Prof. (Dr.) Abhilasha Singh, Professor & Dean Department of English and Foreign Languages at Indira Gandhi National Tribal University, Amarkantak, Madhya Pradesh. M.A. (Gold Medalist), PhD, D.Litt., and UGC-NET qualified in English. She has published three books, various research papers and articles in National and International journals. She has participated and presented several papers at National and International seminars/conferences. Her areas of interest include African-American Literature, Indian English Literature, Women’s Studies and Gender Studies.

speaker Details

Prof. Anita Singh is Professor and Head of the Department of English at Banaras Hindu University. She received a Fulbright-Nehru Visiting Scholar Fellowship for the year 2013-2014 at the University of Virginia, USA. She was a Fellow at the Indian Institute of Advanced Study in Shimla, India (2018–2020). Her recent publications are Staging Feminisms: Gender, Violence, and Performance (Routledge, 2021) and the two edited volumes: Gender, Space, and Resistance: Women and Theatre in India, (DK Print world, 2013) and Evolution of Tradition, Interrogating Transformations in Traditional Folk Performing Arts, (HP Hamilton limited, 2022). She has also served as the conference Chair for The Future of Women conference held in Malaysia (2018), Colombo, Sri Lanka (2019 & 2023), Bangalore (2020) and Bangkok (2024) and is the President of The Asian-African Association for Women, Gender and Sexuality (AAAWGS).

speaker Details

Banibrata Mahanta, Professor of English at Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi. His recent works include English Studies in India: Contemporary and Evolving Paradigms (2019) co-edited with Rajesh Babu Sharma; and the forthcoming Evolving Perspectives in English Studies: Views from the Northeast and Beyond and Narrative Universes of Disability: Global Perspectives. His areas of interest include Indian writing in English, the changing contours of Indian nationalist thought, contemporary literary theory, especially disability studies and rights in India, and translation. An occasional translator, he translates from Bangla, Hindi and Urdu into English. His translation of Kusum Khemani's Lavanyadevi won the PEN/Heim Translation Fund Grant (PEN America) for 2021.

speaker Details

Dr. Mohamed Bataoui is an Assistant Professor of English Language and Cultural Studies at the University of Hassan First in Settat, Morocco. Dr. Bataoui spent ten years teaching English at a high school in Guelmim, Morocco. He has also contributed as a visiting professor at various university centres both within Morocco and internationally. Additionally, Dr. Bataoui earned an International Leaders in Education Diploma from the University of Arizona, USA. His research is characterized by its diversity, reflecting his wide-ranging interests in contemporary postcolonial literature, intercultural studies, critical pedagogy, anti-canonicity and postcolonial feminism.

speaker Details

Dr Mrinmoy Pramanick, Department of Comparative Indian language and literature, University of Calcutta. His areas of interest are translation studies, language and community, migration - refugee and border and Dalit literature. He finished a major project on the Indian exodus from Southeast Asia and worked on a project on Bengali Dalit Oral narratives. He has published, edited and translated eleven books and quite a few articles in reputed journals. He is an executive committee member of the Comparative Literature Association of India and president of Calcutta Comparatists, 1919. His translation of Kalyani Thakur Charal's poems into English I Belong to Nowhere, was published by Tilted Axis, London. He edited Dalit Sahitya Charcha, Lekhalekhir Pathshala, and Kathay Kathay Limbale, Oh Priest Give Me the Seeded Banana, Memoir of Sundarban: A Journey from an Island. Recently he has been awarded with prestigious Sahitya Akademi Translation Prize for 2023.

speaker Details

Goutam Karmakar, Honorary Research Associate at the Faculty of Arts & Design, Durban University of Technology, South Africa. He was awarded the 2024 Alexander von Humboldt Postdoctoral Fellowship and a three-year CHS Postdoctoral Research Position at the Department of English Studies, University of South Africa. Previously, worked at the University of the Western Cape in South Africa as a National Research Foundation postdoctoral fellow as a visiting scholar at the Rachel Carson Centre for Environment & Society, Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich, Germany. His areas of research are South Asian Literature & Culture, Women and Gender Studies, Postcolonial and Decolonial Studies and Environmental Studies.

speaker Details

Priyanka Tripathi is an Associate Professor of English at the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences, Indian Institute of Technology Patna (India). She is also the Co-Executive Editor of the Journal of International Women’s Studies (published by Bridgewater State University). She was awarded a Visiting Research Fellowship (2022-23), by IASH, the University of Edinburgh for her project titled, “Optimizing Caste Intersectionality: A Decolonial Reading of Gender-based Violence in Select Subaltern Fiction in India”. She has published extensively in Indian Literature, Journal of Graphic Novels and Comics, GeoHumanities, and Economic and Political Weekly. Her monograph with Bloomsbury is titled, The Gendered War: Evaluating Feminist Ethnographic Narratives of the 1971 War of Bangladesh. She works in the area of Gender Studies and Indian English Fiction
